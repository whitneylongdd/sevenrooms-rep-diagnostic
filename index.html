Methodology
How each metric is calculated
Click any metric to see the formula, interpretation, and tier thresholds used in the dashboard.
Funnel Win Rate CW ÷ (CW + Loss) ›

The primary health signal. Calculated as closed-won deals divided by all closed deals in the current period. The elite benchmark of 56% is the unweighted average of the 8 highest-performing reps — what’s achievable on this team, not an external industry standard.

TierThresholdInterpretation
Elite≥ 50%Outperforming or at benchmark — protect pipeline, share best practices
Solid35–49%Performing well — fine-tune specific gaps with targeted coaching
At Risk20–34%Meaningful gap — active coaching plan needed within 2 weeks
Critical< 20%Urgent — weekly check-ins, deal reviews, consider PIP assessment
Funnel Utilisation Rate Pitches ÷ SQOs ›

Utilisation measures how efficiently a rep converts sales-qualified opportunities (SQOs) into actual pitches or demos. A low utilisation rate signals reps are qualifying deals but not progressing them — often a prioritisation issue, not a closing problem.

A rep can have a high win rate but low utilisation if they’re cherry-picking easy deals. Conversely, high utilisation with low win rate points to a pitch or close gap rather than sourcing or qualification.

Funnel CW / Rep Total closed-won ÷ rep count ›

Closed-won deals per rep is the volume signal that complements win rate (the efficiency signal). A rep can have a great win rate on a small number of deals. CW/rep ensures we also see who is generating enough deal volume to matter at the quota level.

At the pod and director level, CW/rep helps distinguish volume problems (not enough deals in the funnel) from conversion problems (deals exist but aren’t closing).

Ramp Ramp % Actual CW ÷ Ramp target ›

New reps have a ramp curve — their quota target increases over their first quarters. Ramp % normalises performance to this curve, so a 3-month rep at 90% of their lower target is comparable to a tenured rep at 90% of full quota.

Tenure bandContext
0–6 monthsRamping — lower absolute targets, results directional not definitive
6–12 monthsLate ramp — should be approaching full quota, watch closely
12+ monthsFully ramped — held to full quota, all metrics fully comparable
Gong Overall Gong Score Avg across all scored calls ›

Gong scores each call on a 0–100 scale using conversation patterns: talk time ratio, question frequency, next-step commitment, and other signals. The overall score is averaged across all calls for a rep between Feb 10 and May 11, 2026.

The overall score is a useful fast triage signal, but sub-dimensions are where the coaching insight lives. Reps with fewer than 3 scored calls have their Gong data flagged as limited in the dashboard.

Gong Sub-dimension Scores 8 dimensions, 1–5 scale ›

Each call is scored on 8 dimensions from the team’s scoring rubric. Scores are averaged per rep across all scored calls. The heatmap in the dashboard colours each cell relative to the elite benchmark range.

Research 1–5
Did the rep demonstrate knowledge of the prospect’s business, venue type, and competitive context before the call?
Agenda Setting Yes/No
Did the rep open the call by confirming a shared agenda and gaining buyer agreement before diving in?
Open-Ended Discovery 1–5
Did the rep use open questions to uncover the prospect’s situation and challenges rather than pitching features early?
Quantifying Pain Points 1–5
Did the rep help the prospect put a number on their problem — lost covers, manual hours, revenue leakage — to build urgency?
Goals & Aspirations 1–5
Did the rep connect the product to the prospect’s business goals and future vision, not just current pain? Team average: 1.2/5.
Solution Mapping 1–5
Did the rep tie each feature to a discovered need rather than running a generic product demo?
Objection Handling 1–5
When the prospect raised a concern, did the rep acknowledge, explore, and respond constructively rather than deflecting?
Next Steps 1–5
Did the rep close the call with a specific, time-bound next step that both parties agreed to before hanging up?

Key Findings
What the data says
These are the headline conclusions from the Q2 2026 analysis. Each is addressable — this diagnostic exists to give managers a clear starting point for coaching conversations.
1
Goals & Aspirations is the team’s universal blind spot
Across all reps, Goals & Aspirations averages 1.2 out of 5 — the lowest of any Gong dimension. This measures whether the rep connects the product to the prospect’s forward-looking business goals, not just today’s pain. Without it, deals close on features rather than value, making it harder to justify price and easier for prospects to stall.
“What does success look like for you in 12 months?” — reps who ask this type of question score materially higher on Goals & Aspirations and close at higher rates.
2
Agenda Setting is nearly non-existent across the team
Agenda Setting scores 0.0 to 0.5 for almost every rep. This is the easiest dimension to coach because it’s a behaviour pattern, not a skill ceiling. Reps who set agendas create cleaner calls, surface objections earlier, and spend less time on demos that don’t land. The fix is a two-sentence opener at the top of every call.
3
Win rate variance is structural, not random
The gap between elite and critical reps maps cleanly to funnel stage. Elite reps have higher utilisation and higher Gong scores on Solution Mapping and Next Steps. Critical reps tend to score well early in the funnel but break at pitch or close. Coaching investment has a predictable return: fix the stage where each rep leaks deals.
🔍 Deep Dive: Jack Facecard Conversion
March’s Jack spike was Q1 pull-forward — April is above baseline
March 2026 showed 371 Jack wins — 2.5× the trailing average of 147/month. The bookings raw data revealed the cause: a handful of reps drove the March number through end-of-quarter ELQ acceleration, pulling deals that would have naturally closed in April. When those reps returned to normal pacing in April, the count settled at 170 — which is above the pre-March baseline.

4
Ramp cohort significantly affects team-level metrics
Approximately 30% of the team is still in ramp. When ramp reps are included in team averages, the headline numbers look worse than the underlying picture — these reps haven’t hit their stride yet. The dashboard surfaces ramp status alongside win rate so managers can distinguish structural coaching needs from normal ramp dynamics.
5
Gong coverage is uneven — some coaching conclusions are limited
Not every rep has the same number of scored Gong calls. Reps with fewer than 3 scored calls have noisier sub-dimension data — a single unusual call can skew an average. For these reps, the funnel metrics (win rate, utilisation, CW/rep) are the more reliable coaching anchor. The dashboard flags limited Gong coverage explicitly.
